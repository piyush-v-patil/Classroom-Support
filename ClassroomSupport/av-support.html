🚨 Emergency Call Response Procedure

The intercom in a classroom is primarily used for technical assistance, but it may also be used to notify Classroom Support of an emergency.

Critical: When an instructor calls with an emergency, we must connect them to campus police as quickly as possible.
How to Handle an Emergency Call
  1. If you answer a call over the intercom and learn that there is an emergency, respond over the intercom by saying:
    “A call to 911 is being placed, and 911 will be speaking with you over the intercom shortly.”
  2. From our office phone, dial 7-2200. This will connect you directly to campus police.
  3. When the police answer, say:
    “This is Classroom Support; there is an emergency. Please pick up the intercom in [building and room number]. Please let me know when you are ready to pick up the intercom.”
    (This message can be found on the phone in our office)
  4. Dispatch will use its own TalkMaster console to connect to the intercom in the classroom. Dispatch will acknowledge that they are ready to pick up the intercom.
  5. Once dispatch is ready, end the intercom call with the classroom in your TalkMaster console. Remain on the line with 911 until the call is terminated. Look at the TalkMaster console to see if the status of the room changes to listening, which indicates that 911 is connected to the room.
  6. Lastly, send an email to lst-staff-group@charlotte.edu. In this email, include a description of the call, the building, and the room number of the emergency, and any other relevant details.
